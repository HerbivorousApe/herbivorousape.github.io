Weather This

Weather This is my first attempt to apply MVC design. While I had not worked with MVC before, it always made sense, especially with team supported, enterprise applications.

This is a simple weather app tying to several different APIs to return location based on IP, or zip code, then loading a forecast. Primarily pulling from weather.gov, I found assistive services in ip-api.com and zippopotam.us.

I initially completed a console application and am currently working on a multi-project solution; leaving the console View, moving the Model and Controllers to a ‘Common’ project, and developing a web View. I expect this will soon evolve into my first go at Xamarin.

Mix Tape

Having more thoroughly reacquainted myself with HTML/CSS/JS and exploring the MERN stack; I turned to bolstering my .NET experience. While I worked supporting dozens of applications, I had yet to really apply the fundamentals and create a project of my own from the ground up.

I spun up a quick Mad Lib console application, but wanted to expand a bit. A menu was added, as well as Escape the Maze (text-based adventure), and Wheel of Misfortune, a ‘guess the phrase’ type game. The resulting conglomeration is titled “Mix Tape”.

This is nothing award winning, but I was happy to get an original creation stamped out. Also, happy that I had retained much of what I learned as a Software Engineer. Looking at the code, there are things I would do differently today – and there are some glaring bugs. As I am moving forward, I’m content to let it stand where it is.

BUGS!

While studying for my Front End Libraries certification I decided to take a break to apply some of the covered material. I wanted to create a game.

What I settled on was BUGS! a very basic, somewhat buggy, whack-a-mole type. Ten hours, and about 300 lines of code later, I had it.

This was an interesting exercise, allowing me to reacquaint with jQuery – which I had not used since my webmaster days, stretch my vanilla JavaScript muscles, and play around with incorporating other libraries – something I typically avoid if I can help it.

I learned I’d rather work without jQuery, and that I really like Bootstrap. Animate.css was unnecessary, but helpful – Font Awesome was even less necessary, and less helpful. Though, I am happy to have the experience with all of it.

Short of a few code snippets in my old toolbox, this was my first real experience using setInverval to step through functions, which, is incredibly simple, incredibly powerful.

I am especially happy with the dynamically created grid, the, (admittedly imperfect, but present), scalability, randomized animations pulling from arrays. I recall overcoming challenges such as being able to continue accumulating points after time ran out, and being able to squish bugs when paused.

Looking at it now there are things I would do differently – I still have an itch to expand functionality, and may one day. For now, it’s my first non-text-based game. I built it from scratch. I learned a lot, including a bit more about of what I am capable.
